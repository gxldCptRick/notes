Airport Web App

The LMS Assignment

  • Requirements
  • What to watch out for

We got an assignment that basically is to create a flight website. So we will need to create users and any user that registers with our site will be an admin. Most of his document he refers to users as anyone who is actually going to our site whether or not they are currently logged in. Admins are just users who are logged in. That means that anybody who signs up to the site will be able to create flights delete flights and etc... We all agreed that in a real app this would be hilariously bad.

  • Everyone should be able to purchase a ticket on the website
  • Only admins will be able to create flights to make purchases from
  • The airports will be a predefined set of airports and does not need to change during the runtime of the application
  • When signing up a user you need to have all the following: full name, email, street address, phone, the number of seats the are purchasing, and payment info

Gotchas to look out for

  • The Timezones
  • Credit Cards
The Timezones

So for the timezone be careful because he wants the arrival and departure times to be in the timezone of the airport that they are going to travel to. So that means if you are going from Salt Lake City to New York you will have to have the departure time be in MST and the arrival should be in EST. He said that we can use any external libraries for helping with the date-time stuff

Credit Cards

So for credit cards we have to keep them secure so that means no storing the fake CSV's and only storing the last 4 digits of the card. Mr. Cox is very insistent that we do not use any real credit card information. He also told us that to validate it would be just making sure that it isn't expired and that its number starts with a 9 so it should be easy enough to work.
